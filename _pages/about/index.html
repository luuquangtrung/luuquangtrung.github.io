I am a maître de conférences (equivalently associate professor) at Paris-Saclay University, France, affiliated with the Multimedia and Networking (MULTINET) team of the Laboratory of Signals and Systems (L2S) of CentraleSupélec for research and the graduate engineering school Polytech Paris-Saclay for teaching activities.

I obtained a Ph.D from CentraleSupélec - Paris-Saclay University, France, in 2022. My thesis was also realized at Nokia Bell Labs France, within the CIFRE Ph.D Fellowship program supported by the French government (ANRT). My thesis was awarded the 2022 Best PhD Dissertation Award on Distributed Systems and Networks by the French Research Community on Networks and Distributed Systems, and ACM SigOps France.

Prior to joining Paris-Saclay, I was a lecturer at the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Hanoi University of Science and Technology (2023 - 2025), and postdoctoral fellow at the University of Avignon and the French National Centre for Scientific Research (LAAS-CNRS), Toulouse, France, France (2021 - 2023).

My research focuses on improving resource management in next-generation communication networks (5G and beyond), leveraging mathematical optimization and artificial intelligence.
